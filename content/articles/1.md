# Governor Malvenux brings cad to departments
![Photo of governor with dispatcher](/images/governordispatcher.png)

Phoenix City takes a major step forward in public safety as Governor Malvenux officially rolls out a brand-new Computer Aided Dispatch (CAD) system, modernizing how the city's emergency services communicate and respond. In this exclusive interview with Phoenix City News, the Governor breaks down what the new system means for law enforcement, first responders, and the community at large. below we interviewed the governor on his insight into the new cad system.

\[**Interviewer**\]: Governor Mal, what inspired you to push for a new CAD system, and what was the moment you knew it was time to make this happen?
> \[**Governor Malvenux**\]: Ive always backed and increased funding for our Boys and Girls in Blue, with the random and unorganised rise in crime across Phoenix, further tools to help root out chaos and anarchy were required.

\[**Interviewer**\]: What was the state of emergency communications before this, and how did that shape your vision for improvement?
> \[**Governor**\]: Emergency communications were mostly Officer driven, with records not corroborating across departments and not as streamlined, that was the main inspiration that change was needed, and its been recieved well.

\[**Interviewer**\]: How long has your team been working on bringing this system to life?
> My team and inner circle have been working tirelessly at this for weeks to make sure these changes and improvements hit at the right time and work accordingly.

\[**Interviewer**\]: For readers who may not be familiar, in plain terms, what does this CAD system do and how does it change day-to-day operations for our first responders?
> \[**Governor**\]: CAD means that officers are assigned to calls appropriately, ensuring quick response time and resources are managed correctly, it makes records cross departmental, and easily accessible for any officer. However safety features to stop officer abuse is being introduced. It also aligns and shows what Officers have as certifications and specialisations, meaning that pursuits and riots are engaged by trained officers.

\[**Interviewer**\]: Which departments are currently plugged into the system, and are there plans to bring more agencies on board?
> \[**Governor**\]: All departments are plugged in, however we are hoping to see non LEO departments make more use of the CAD in the coming months.

\[**Interviewer**\]: What’s the most exciting feature of this system that you think people will notice right away?

> \[**Governor**\]: Most certainly the records feature, it shows with ease of access, a persons records, such as past crimes and cautions, allowing officers to police smarter, and keep our streets safe.

\[**Interviewer**\]: How do you expect Phoenix City residents to feel the difference, even if they never see the system directly?
> \[**Governor**\]: The change won't be noticable just yet, but when Phoenix is in the books of History, or in monthly crime stats, the change will begin to show as officers will be able to crack down on criminals even further.

\[**Interviewer**\]: What does this mean for officer coordination and response times on the ground?
> \[**Governor**\]: This at a minimum should be improving officer response times by 60 seconds, critics argue that 60 seconds matters little, however in my time in the army, and stories ive heard from our departments, every second is valuable.

\[**Interviewer**\]: Governor, would you say this is one of the bigger public safety wins of your administration so far?
> \[**Governor**\]: I would say so, cracking down on crime is imperative, however when young people turn to crime, its very rarely their first choice of employment, im working with my team to write up a bill for a jobs program across Phoenix, to target felons and at risk youths into getting employment and seeing how co operating with the law is the smart choice.
